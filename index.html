Password Anomaly Explorer

This site starts with a library of “normal” passwords—both preloaded examples and at least ten you add—to give its tiny AI model called an autoencoder enough data to learn typical patterns. When you test a new password, the site can rebuild it: a low reconstruction error marks it as Normal, while a high error flags it as Anomalous.

An anomaly is something that doesn’t fit the usual pattern or expectation—an outlier or “odd one out.” In our password explorer, an anomalous password is one the tiny AI model couldn’t rebuild very well because it looked different from the training examples.
